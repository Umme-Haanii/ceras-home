Which tiles are more suitable for your home,your office, your hospital and your any other space. So, here are the differences between your favourite tiles. You can select from here!

CERAMIC TILES

Ceramic tiles are relatively easy to clean and maintain.
Ceramic tiles are very strong, durable, and abrasion-resistant.
Ceramic tiles are much more affordable than natural stones like marble, granite, etc.
They can be brittle and prone to cracking or chipping if a heavy object is dropped on them or if they are subjected to significant impact.
In colder climates or during the winter months, ceramic tiles can feel cold underfoot.
In rooms with hard surfaces, ceramic tiles can contribute to increased noise levels due to their reflective properties.

GLAZED VITRIFIED TILES

Glazed Vitrified tiles are easy to clean and maintain.
Glazed tiles are hard, virtually non-porous and impervious to stains and scratches, making them a great option for indoors as well as outdoors.
Glazed Vitrified tiles have rich color patterns, many specifications, easy cleaning, large selection space, and are suitable for kitchens and bathrooms.
A significant disadvantage of glazed vitrified tiles is that it is more expensive than ceramic tiles.
Glazed Vitrified tiles, especially glossy finishes, can be slippery when wet.
The Glazed vitrified tiles have less strength and life.

POLISHED VITRIFIED TILES

Polished Vitrified Tiles are much easier to maintain as compared to other natural stone flooring.
Polished Vitrified tiles are extremely durable, making them suitable for high-traffic areas.
Since they have undergone a vitrification process,polished vitrified tiles are less porous, this means they absorb less moisture and water.
Polished Vitrified tiles are more expensive than other types of tiles like ceramic or porcelain.
Polished Vitrified tiles can be slippery, especially when wet, making them less suitable for areas that require slip-resistant surfaces like bathrooms or kitchens.

ABOUT MATTE FINISH TILES

They Are Non-Slippery: Matte tiles are non-slippery as they have better friction than gloss tiles. This quality makes them the go-to options for bathrooms and wet areas. Matte floor tiles are also a better option from the safety point of view if you have kids, senior citizens and pets living with you.

ABOUT POLISHED VITRIFIED TILES

Polished vitrified tiles are hard and resistant to wear, making them suitable for most indoor spaces except for bathrooms and kitchens. They can also be used for balconies and exterior wall decoration. By using infiltration technology, polished vitrified tiles can imitate various effects of stone or wood.

ABOUT GLAZED VIRTIFIED TILES

The non-porous nature of glazed vitrified tiles makes them a perfect fit for spaces with high humidity, such as bathrooms and kitchens, as they won't absorb water. The smooth, glossy surface of glazed vitrified tiles means easy cleaning and minimal effort to keep them looking immaculate.

ABOUT CERAMIC TILES

Ceramic tiles are most often used as a decorative feature wall in the home, making for an easy to clean and maintain surface finish for all spaces, from the kitchen and laundry room to the bathroom and more. Depending on the specific type and collection of ceramic tiles, some options can be used as a flooring solution in low traffic areas however most ceramic tile collections are best used as a wall tile.

ABOUT ANTI STATIC TILES

Anti-static floor tiles prevent or reduce the accumulation of static charge that builds upon a routine basis in any area. It also transfers the static charge through the floor to the ground. Thereby anti-static tiles avoid any mishaps, shocks or accidents and ensure the safety of all workers and visitors.
